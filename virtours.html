Virtours

UX DESIGN, VIRTUAL REALITY

Virtours is a VR application, which suggests attractions, helps with detailed trip planning, and allows users to book hotels, all on the same platform.

ROLE

UX Researcher, Product Designer

TEAM

Individual

Introduction

There are many trip planning methods and options online, but with the latest technology innovations, such as VR/AR, there is a new potential to revolutionize how trips are planned and experienced. Virtours is a VR trip planner, which provides a new dimension for travelers, allowing them to experience destinations before they make the final decision on where to go.

Project Goal

The primary goal of the application is to create a seamless booking experience, where the user is able to explore various destinations and hotels in VR, check out local sights, and seek out key information. It is important that the experience is not stuck in high-end VR goggles, but also can be used on a wide variety of channels.

User Interviews

I conducted user interviews to learn more about people’s trip planning process and which tools they normally used. I enquired about how people choose their travel destinations, their biggest obstacles, and what information, or features, they need when planning trips. Here are some of the key take-aways from the results:

Trip Planning Process

While conducting the the interviews, I observed people’s planning process and classified their steps into separate stages.

How the trip planning process benefits from VR

Most people consume a lot of time deciding and finalizing where to go. I therefore started to research how VR can be used to simplify the process of gathering information and help to make the final decision on where to go easier.

User Persona

Based on the information above, I developed my user persona to serve as a cumulative representation of the common user goals and pain points, that I derived from the user interviews.

    • Kyle loves to travel and often finds himself reading travel books and blogs to find his next ultimate travel adventure. Most of the time he plans his trip in advance, and prepares all necessary gear before he sets off.

    Goals

    • Comprehensively understand and get a better “feel” of a destination before visiting it.

    • Have a convenient and efficient way to gather information and find pictures and videos of the place to look at.

    Pain points

    • Find it troublesome to keep different applications open at the same time when planning a trip.

Wireframe & Prototype

I started to sketch out wireframes, utilizing the trip planning process, as a guide. After conducting usability tests, I created the prototype with A-Frame, which is supported on multiple platforms, including Google Cardboard, Oculus Rift, and all modern browsers.

Landing page

The landing page has a search bar and location cards, which help the user to discover attractions more easily.

Destination page

    There are few, but critical pieces of information, such as the destination name, live weather, distance and top sites to visit. Also the 360º panorama images allow the users to imagine themselves at the travel destination.

Site details page

On the site details page, a 3D terrain model provides the user with an in-depth understanding of the location.

Select must see places

Users can pin various locations on a map, which are automatically added to the trip itinerary.

Pick hotels

Users can view the hotel in VR and select the time of the stay. There is also a card-list on the right-hand side, showing other hotels, which the users can scroll through.

Live itinerary

Users can view a pre-generated itinerary with 360º panorama images, location and dates. Users can also share the itinerary with others.
